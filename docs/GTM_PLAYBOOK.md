# Visible to AI — GTM Playbook

Distilled from Christian Pavarelli's early-stage GTM framework. This is the
filter every product, copy, and channel decision must pass before we ship it.

If we can't answer a question on this page in one sentence, we don't ship the
thing it gates.

---

## Phase 1 — The Rule of One

Before we touch a line of UI code, each of these must have a one-sentence answer:

| | Answer in one sentence |
|---|---|
| **One customer** | A specific business owner — industry, size, role, geo. Not "small businesses." |
| **One pain** | The #1 thing they lose sleep over. In their words. |
| **One product** | What we hand them. Not features — the deliverable. |
| **One outcome** | The dream they walk away with. |
| **One channel** | Where we reach them. Not "social" — a specific place. |
| **One business model** | One pricing model, one price. |

If any row is blank, we are not ready to ship.

---

## Phase 2 — Customer discovery (BEFORE we rewrite anything)

Pick **3 candidate customer segments**. Rank each against:

- **Pain awareness** — do they already know AI matters to their business?
- **Reachability** — do we know where they hang out (online or offline)?
- **Ability to pay** — can they drop $29–$99/mo without a meeting?
- **Volume** — are there millions of them?

Pick one. Run **10 discovery calls** on it. **No selling.**

### The 15-minute discovery script

1. "Tell me about your business — what do you do, who's your customer?"
2. "When someone wants what you sell, how do they find businesses like yours today?"
3. "Have you ever asked ChatGPT or AI about your industry? What happened?"
4. "If AI is recommending other businesses instead of yours to your potential customers, what would you do?"
5. "Of these three things [list candidate pains], which one keeps you up most at night?"
6. "If a tool showed you exactly what AI says about you and what to fix, what would that be worth?"
7. "Can I reach back out when we have something to test?"

After 10 calls we know: the customer, the pain (ranked), the outcome (in their
words), the price they would pay.

---

## Phase 3 — Landing page (from discovery output, not from our heads)

Build sections in this exact order:

1. **The promise** — hero, one sentence, the dream outcome in their words
2. **The 3 benefits** — laddered outcomes
3. **The features** — what we actually do that delivers those benefits
4. **How it works** — 3 steps, plain English
5. **FAQ / objections** — the top 5 objections we heard on calls, answered

If a section doesn't tie back to something a real business owner said on a
discovery call, it gets cut.

---

## Phase 4 — First 100 customers via outbound

The proven early-stage channel: outbound + email. Three steps:

1. **List** — 100 people who match the ICP (LinkedIn search, niche communities,
   forums, offline if that's where they actually are)
2. **Enrich** — for each: name, business, recent activity (last LinkedIn post,
   business news, etc.), contact (email + LinkedIn URL)
3. **Message** — 3 lines, in this shape:
   - Line 1: highly personalized — about *them* (their post, their business)
   - Line 2: short compliment + the hook
   - Line 3: direct ask — "Would you be open to a 15-min call about how AI
     represents your business?"

Expected funnel: 100 sent → ~30 read → ~5 reply → ~2 calls happen. That's
normal. It's a funnel, not a personal judgement.

---

## Phase 5 — The 8 questions we must answer before we ship the rewrite

1. Who is the customer? (Persona — industry, size, role, geo.)
2. What's their #1 pain? (Their words.)
3. What's the promise? (Dream outcome.)
4. What are the 3 benefits?
5. What are the top 3 objections?
6. Where do we find them? (One channel.)
7. What do we charge? (One model, one price.)
8. What does one use of the product give them?

Any "we don't know yet" → no rewrite. Back to discovery.

---

## Rules — what we will NOT do

- Build features before we have a customer talking to us.
- Run paid ads before the landing page converts manual traffic.
- Talk in our voice — every word on the page comes from a discovery call.
- Use "portfolio," "client," "agency," "strategist," or "Share-of-Model"
  anywhere a business owner can see.
- Sell during discovery — only ask questions.
- Chase more than one customer segment at a time.

---

## Why this exists

Visible to AI is for **business owners** asking "is my business visible to AI?"
— not for agencies managing other people's businesses. The agency framing
slipped in via copy and onboarding language, not via product decisions. This
playbook keeps that drift from happening again: every UI decision has to map
back to something a real business owner said on a discovery call.

The product name is fixed: **Visible to AI**. The promise is in the name.
